Our research spans two categories — from fundamental astrophysics to applied high-energy and radiation studies — through theory and multi-messenger observations.

## Fundamental Astrophysics {#fundamental}

Uncovering the physics of the high-energy universe — black holes and MeV gamma rays — through theory and multi-messenger observations.

### Black Hole Astrophysics {#blackhole}

Building on the first measurement of magnetic fields in AGN coronae (Inoue & Doi 2018),
we combine ALMA millimeter observations, IXPE X-ray polarimetry, and XRISM high-resolution
spectroscopy to reveal the physics of plasma, magnetic fields, and particle acceleration
in the immediate vicinity of black holes.

### High-Energy Astrophysics (MeV Gamma Rays) {#mev}

The MeV band is the last frontier of observational astronomy.
We participate in NASA's COSI mission and the GRAMS balloon project, forecasting the MeV sky,
studying particle acceleration in AGN coronae, hunting the sources of IceCube neutrinos,
and investigating blazar populations.

## Applied High-Energy and Radiation Studies {#applied}

Applying our expertise in high-energy astrophysics to real-world radiation challenges of the space age.

### Lunar Radiation Environment {#lunar}

For the coming era of sustained human activity on the Moon, we apply the tools of high-energy
astrophysics to understand and forecast the radiation environment produced by galactic cosmic rays
and solar energetic particles on the lunar surface.
